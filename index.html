Case Study 2: Virtual Ticket Backlog Clearance

Situation: During a peak sales season, the virtual support queue in Zendesk accumulated over 200 unresolved tickets, causing response times to slip to 48 hours.
Action: I analyzed the ticket tags and realized 40% of inquiries were about the same pricing update. I drafted a clear macro response explaining the update and collaborated with management to send a bulk email notification. I then focused manually on the remaining complex tickets.
Result: Cleared the entire backlog within 3 days. Reduced incoming ticket volume by 40% for that week. CSAT for the week remained at 97%.

Case Study 3: Turning Support into Opportunity

Situation: A customer contacted support via chat only to fix a minor technical issue with their current service.
Action: While resolving the technical issue in Zendesk, I noticed their usage data in HubSpot indicated they were hitting their limits. I politely informed them that a different service tier would solve this permanently and be more efficient for their needs.
Result: The customer adjusted their service immediately. Demonstrated the value of proactive support in driving customer growth.

Case Study 4: System Outage Communication

Situation: The company portal went down unexpectedly during business hours, causing a flood of calls and walk-ins.
Action: I took ownership of communication. I created a standardized script for the front desk team and updated the Zendesk auto-responder to acknowledge the outage and provide an estimated fix time. I personally updated every waiting client every 30 minutes.
